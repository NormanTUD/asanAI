Key idea: Before any distinction, there is the void — undifferentiated nothingness. The moment you draw a boundary (a circle, a line), you create two states: the marked (inside) and the unmarked (outside). The mark — Spencer-Brown's "cross" ⌐ — is both the act of distinction and the name of the marked state.

2. Axiom 1 — The Law of Calling

Calling a name twice is the same as calling it once. Redundant distinctions collapse.

Formal: ⌐⌐ = ⌐  (mark mark = mark)

Analogy: Saying "it's hot, it's hot, it's hot" conveys the same information as saying "it's hot" once. Repeating a distinction side-by-side doesn't change the value. Any number of adjacent marks = one mark.

3. Axiom 2 — The Law of Crossing

Crossing a boundary twice returns you to where you started. A mark inside a mark cancels out.

Key insight: Nesting marks is like crossing a boundary repeatedly.
• Even depth (0, 2, 4…) → you're back outside → Unmarked State
• Odd depth (1, 3, 5…) → you're inside → Marked State
This is the foundation of all oscillation and self-reference in the calculus.

5. Mapping to Logic — Boolean Interpretation

Spencer-Brown showed his calculus is equivalent to Boolean logic. The mark = True, the unmarked state = False. The cross acts as NOT, and adjacency (calling) acts as OR.

Operations in Laws of Form notation:

Operation LoF Notation Boolean Result
Notation key:
• ⌐(p) = NOT p (crossing/negation)
• p q = p OR q (calling/adjacency)
• ⌐(p q) = NOT(p OR q) = NOR(p,q)
• ⌐(⌐(p) ⌐(q)) = NOT(NOT p OR NOT q) = p AND q
• ⌐(p) q = (NOT p) OR q = p → q (implication)

6. Re-entry — Self-Reference & Imaginary Values

What happens when a form refers to itself? Spencer-Brown introduced re-entry: a mark whose inside is… itself. This creates oscillation — an "imaginary" logical value, analogous to √(−1).

t=0
The equation f = ⌐(f) has no fixed Boolean solution. If f is marked, then ⌐(f) is unmarked — contradiction! Spencer-Brown's resolution: f oscillates between marked and unmarked over time, creating a third value — the imaginary state. This is the seed of time, oscillation, and self-reference in the calculus. It anticipates concepts in cybernetics, autopoiesis, and the foundations of computation.
