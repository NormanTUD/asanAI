Attention = Points That Pull Each Other Closer

Every word is a point in space. Attention makes each point move toward the points it finds relevant. The simplicial complex shows you which points clump together — that's the "meaning" attention creates.

What You're Seeing

The analogy: Imagine words are people at a party. Each person looks around (Q) and sees name-tags (K). If your "looking" matches someone's "tag," you walk toward them. After one round, everyone has moved to the average position of the people they found interesting.

Simplicial complex = the friend-groups that form. An edge means "these two found each other interesting." A triangle means "these three all found each other interesting" — they formed a tight group.
Step 1: Each word starts at a position (its meaning).
x = [gender, being, power]
Step 2: Measure "relevance" between all pairs.
score(i,j) = how much word i cares about word j
This is Q·K — just a (possibly biased) way to measure similarity.
Step 3: Each word moves to the weighted center of all words, weighted by relevance.
new position of word i = Σ (relevance to j) × (position of j)
This is the V part — the actual movement.
Result: Words that are "relevant" to each other end up near each other. That cluster = a simplex = composed meaning.
