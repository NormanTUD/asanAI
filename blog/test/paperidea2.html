⚡ Section 5: Inner Layers Compute, Outer Layers Translate

The first and last layers translate between token space and the model's internal geometric language. The middle layers do the heavy computation — and the last layer often reverses the deformation.

Observation: Inner layers show the highest deformation magnitude (Jacobian norm), while the last layer often "unmorphs" the space back toward the embedding geometry so the language modeling head can read the result.

This is consistent with the conjecture that the model's internal geometric language is different from the token embedding space, and layers 1 and L serve as translators.
