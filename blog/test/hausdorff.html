T₁ — Fréchet Space

For any two distinct points, each has a neighborhood not containing the other. But they may overlap!

Rule: For distinct x, y: ∃ open U with x ∈ U, y ∉ U and ∃ open V with y ∈ V, x ∉ V. But U ∩ V can be non-empty!

T₀ — Kolmogorov Space

At least ONE of the two points has a neighborhood not containing the other. Weakest separation!

Rule: For distinct x, y: ∃ open U such that (x ∈ U, y ∉ U) or (y ∈ U, x ∉ U). Only one direction needed!

First Countable Spaces

Every point has a countable neighborhood basis. Drag the point and watch the nested basis shrink in.

Rule: At every point x, there is a countable collection {B₁, B₂, B₃, …} of open neighborhoods such that every open set containing x contains some Bₙ.
Drag the point around. The nested balls form a countable local basis.

Second Countable Spaces

The whole topology has a countable basis. See how rational-center balls cover ℝ².

Rule: There exists a countable collection of open sets such that every open set is a union of sets from this collection. Example: open balls with rational centers and rational radii in ℝⁿ.
Click to place an open set. Watch it get covered by countable basis elements.
