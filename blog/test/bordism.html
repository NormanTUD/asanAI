What is a Bordism?

A bordism (or cobordism) between two manifolds M₁ and M₂ is a manifold W whose boundary is the disjoint union of M₁ and M₂.

Think of it as: M₁ and M₂ are the "ends" and W is the "stuff in between" connecting them.

🔵 Cylinder: A circle bordant to itself — the simplest case. Nothing changes.

👖 Pair of Pants: One circle splits into two. This is like multiplication in a TQFT.

🧢 Cap: A circle bounds a disk — it's bordant to the empty set ∅. The circle "dies."

🥚 Birth: The reverse — a circle appears from nothing.

💡 Key Insight: Two manifolds are bordant if together they form the boundary of some higher-dimensional manifold. Bordism is an equivalence relation!

🧮 Bordism groups: The set of manifolds modulo bordism forms an abelian group under disjoint union. The pair-of-pants gives a ring structure!
