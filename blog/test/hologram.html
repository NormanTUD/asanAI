🧠 Why This Matters for LLMs

In a Transformer, the concept "dog" is NOT stored in dimension #42. It's stored as a pattern across all 768+ dimensions — exactly like a hologram stores a scene across its entire plate.

Zero out random dimensions → the concept gets noisier, but never fully disappears. This is holographic graceful degradation.

The signal quality follows: Quality ≈ √(k / d) where k = surviving dimensions, d = total. Even at 50% damage, you retain ~71% of the signal — enough to still identify any concept.

This is why LLMs are so robust: knowledge is distributed holographically across every weight and dimension, not stored in fragile individual neurons.
