📐 Traditional Proof vs. Formal Proof

Traditional Proof

  • ✍️ Written for human understanding
  • ⏭️ Routine steps omitted
  • 🧠 Relies on reader's context
  • 🎨 Uses intuitive arguments
  • ⚠️ Susceptible to logical gaps

Formal Proof

  • 🖥️ Written for machine verification
  • ✅ Every inference checked
  • 📋 All assumptions explicit
  • 🔗 Traced back to axioms
  • 🛡️ Extremely reliable

📅 Milestones in Formal Proof

Historical Timeline (click to expand)

1954
First Computer Proof — M. Davis proves sum of two even numbers is even on "Johniac"
Programmed the Presburger algorithm for additive arithmetic at the Institute for Advanced Study.
1956
Principia Mathematica Automated — Wang's procedure generates proofs of every theorem in predicate calculus
By end of 1959, automation of Russell and Whitehead's work was substantially complete.
1968
Automath — N.G. de Bruijn designs first program to check general mathematical proofs
Eventually checked every proposition in Landau's primer on Dedekind cuts.
1996
Robbins Conjecture — Fully automated proof by EQP (8 days of computation)
No human assistance, no specialized algorithms. Just type the problem and wait.
2004
Four-Color Theorem — Gonthier's formal proof in Coq
One of the most meticulously verified proofs in history. Based on Robertson et al.'s proof.
2008
Prime Number Theorem — Both elementary and analytic proofs formalized
Harrison formalized the analytic proof in HOL Light; Avigad et al. did the Selberg-Erdős proof in Isabelle.
