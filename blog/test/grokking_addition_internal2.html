What is the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT)?

PREREQUISITE

Problem: You have a list of 113 numbers (like pixel brightnesses, or neuron weights). You want to know: "Is there a hidden repeating pattern in this list?"

You hear a chord on a piano. The DFT tells you which individual notes (frequencies) make up that chord, and how loud each note is.

The DFT decomposes any list of numbers into a sum of sine and cosine waves at different frequencies. Watch:

Blue = your signal (sum of waves). Colored = the individual frequency components the DFT finds.

The DFT is just asking: "For each possible frequency k = 1, 2, 3, ..., how much does my signal look like cos(2πk·n/N) and sin(2πk·n/N)?" If the answer is "a lot!", that frequency is present in your signal.

Why does the transformer care? Modular addition repeats every 113 steps. The DFT is the natural tool for anything periodic. The transformer's weights, after training, become sparse in the Fourier basis — meaning only ~5 frequencies have significant energy. Everything else is ~zero.

Trig Identities — Why They Matter Here

THE KEY MATH

The Goal: We have cos(ω·a) and sin(ω·a) and cos(ω·b) and sin(ω·b). We want cos(ω·(a+b)) and sin(ω·(a+b)).

But wait — the network needs to MULTIPLY cos(ω·a) × cos(ω·b). Neural networks are mostly linear (matrix multiplications + additions). Where does the multiplication come from? That's what attention does...

What is the "OV Circuit"?

Each attention head has 4 matrices: Q (query), K (key), V (value), O (output). The OV circuit = O × V determines what information gets moved.

Q and K → "Where to look"

The Query asks "what am I looking for?" The Key says "here's what I have." Their dot product = attention score.

O and V → "What to copy"

The Value is the actual content. O reshapes it into the right format. Together, OV = the information pipeline.

The OV circuit for Head 0: it reads sin/cos at frequency k=35 from the input, and outputs sin/cos at the same frequency. The attention weight then multiplies this, creating the cross-terms we need.
Key finding from the paper: The softmax in the attention is operating in a nearly LINEAR regime (97.5% variance explained by a linear fit). So attention_weight ≈ 0.5 + α·(cos(ω·a) - cos(ω·b)). This makes the whole thing a clean bilinear product.

Step 3: Constructive Interference — How the Answer Emerges

THE MAGIC

After the MLP computes cos(ω_k·(a+b)) for each of 5 frequencies, the unembedding matrix computes a score for every possible answer c:

25
47
5
Colored = individual cos waves per frequency. White = their SUM. The peak at the correct answer c* is where ALL waves equal 1 simultaneously.

Why does this work?

At c* = correct answer, all 5 cosines = 1 (they add up). At any wrong c, they point in random directions and mostly cancel.
With just 1 frequency, cos(ω₁₄·(72-c)) ≈ 0.998 at c=64 — almost indistinguishable from the true peak at c=72! But with 5 frequencies, only c=72 gets ALL five cosines = 1. The wrong answers get random mixtures that cancel out.

Step 4: Softmax — Turning the Peak into a Probability

SOFTMAX

Softmax takes a list of numbers (logits) and turns them into probabilities that sum to 1. The biggest number gets the most probability.
