📐 Core Equations

1. Scaled Dot-Product Attention

\[ \text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V \]

2. Query, Key, Value Projections

\[ Q = XW^Q, \quad K = XW^K, \quad V = XW^V \] \[ W^Q, W^K, W^V \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{model} \times d_k} \]

3. RoPE Positional Embedding

\[ R_{\Theta,m} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos(m\theta_1) & -\sin(m\theta_1) & 0 \\ \sin(m\theta_1) & \cos(m\theta_1) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \] \[ \theta_i = 10000^{-2i/d} \] \[ \tilde{q}_m = R_{\Theta,m} \cdot q_m, \quad \tilde{k}_n = R_{\Theta,n} \cdot k_n \]

4. Attention Score with RoPE

\[ a_{mn} = \frac{\tilde{q}_m^T \tilde{k}_n}{\sqrt{d_k}} = \frac{(R_{\Theta,m} q_m)^T (R_{\Theta,n} k_n)}{\sqrt{d_k}} \] \[ = \frac{q_m^T R_{\Theta,m-n} k_n}{\sqrt{d_k}} \]

↑ RoPE encodes relative position (m−n) into the attention score!

5. Simplicial Complex from Attention

\[ \text{0-simplex (vertex):} \quad \sigma_0 = \{v_i\} \quad \text{(each token embedding)} \] \[ \text{1-simplex (edge):} \quad \sigma_1 = \{v_i, v_j\} \quad \text{if } \alpha_{ij} > \epsilon \] \[ \text{2-simplex (triangle):} \quad \sigma_2 = \{v_i, v_j, v_k\} \quad \text{if } \alpha_{ij}, \alpha_{jk}, \alpha_{ik} > \epsilon \]

The simplicial complex \(\mathcal{K}\) captures how attention "focuses meaning locally" — tokens that mutually attend form higher-dimensional simplices representing composed meaning.

💡 How to Read This Visualization

What you're seeing:

  • Points (0-simplices): Each token's embedding in 3D semantic space. The position encodes meaning along three axes.
  • Edges (1-simplices): Drawn between tokens where mutual attention weight exceeds threshold ε. Thicker = stronger attention.
  • Triangles (2-simplices): Formed when three tokens all mutually attend to each other above ε. These represent composed meaning — a local semantic "focus region."
  • RoPE rotation: Shown as arrows from original to rotated positions. Nearby tokens rotate similarly, preserving relative geometry.
  • Attention heatmap: Shows the full attention matrix α_{ij} after softmax.

Key insight: Attention doesn't just "look at" other tokens — it creates a simplicial complex that defines a local topological structure of meaning. A 2-simplex (triangle) between "king", "lion", "power" means these three concepts are being jointly composed by the attention mechanism into a unified semantic region.
