🌟 What is Shor's Algorithm?

Shor's Algorithm is a quantum algorithm that can factor large numbers exponentially faster than any known classical algorithm. This matters because modern encryption (RSA) relies on the difficulty of factoring large numbers.

🔢 Step 1: The Factoring Problem

Given a composite number N, find two non-trivial factors p and q such that N = p × q.

This is easy for small numbers (you can see that 15 = 3 × 5), but becomes incredibly hard for numbers with hundreds of digits. The best classical algorithms take sub-exponential time.

⚡ Step 2: Classical vs Quantum Speed

The key insight of Shor's algorithm is that factoring can be reduced to finding the period of a function. While classical computers struggle with period-finding for large numbers, quantum computers can do it efficiently using quantum superposition and interference.

🐌 Classical (Trial Division)

Time: O(√N)

For a 2048-bit number:

~10³⁰⁰ operations

Longer than the age of the universe!

🚀 Quantum (Shor's)

Time: O((log N)³)

For a 2048-bit number:

~10¹⁰ operations

Minutes to hours!

🔄 Step 3: The Period-Finding Reduction

Shor's key insight: factoring N is equivalent to finding the period r of the function f(x) = ax mod N, where 'a' is a randomly chosen number coprime to N.

f(x) = ax mod N
Find smallest r such that ar ≡ 1 (mod N)

Once we find the period r, we can (usually) find factors using:

GCD(ar/2 - 1, N) and GCD(ar/2 + 1, N)

🌀 Step 5: Quantum Fourier Transform

The Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) is the secret weapon. It converts the periodic pattern in the quantum state into a measurable frequency, revealing the period!

Think of it like this: if you have a musical note (periodic wave), the Fourier Transform tells you its frequency. QFT does the same thing but for quantum states.

QFT|x⟩ = (1/√N) Σk e2πixk/N |k⟩
