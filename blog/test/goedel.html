Summary — The Big Ideas

  1. First Theorem: Any consistent formal system strong enough to express arithmetic contains true statements that cannot be proved within it.
  2. Gödel Numbering: Statements can be encoded as numbers, letting the system reason about its own sentences.
  3. Self-Reference: A sentence can be constructed that says "I am not provable," creating an inescapable dilemma.
  4. Trade-off: You must sacrifice either completeness or consistency — you cannot have both.
  5. Second Theorem: A consistent system cannot prove its own consistency from within.
