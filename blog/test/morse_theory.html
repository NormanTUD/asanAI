Morse Theory

An Interactive Visual Exploration

Morse theory reveals a profound connection: the shape (topology) of a space can be completely understood by studying a single smooth function on it — specifically, by examining its critical points (peaks, valleys, and saddles).

The Core Idea: As you raise a "water level" across a landscape, the topology of the submerged region changes only when the water reaches a critical point. Between critical points, nothing topologically interesting happens.

4. Building Topology: Cell Attachment

Morse theory says: passing through a critical point of index k is equivalent to "attaching a k-cell" (a k-dimensional disk) to the existing space. This is how the manifold is built up piece by piece.

Summary

Morse Theory in one sentence: The topology of a smooth manifold is completely determined by the critical points (and their indices) of any generic smooth function on it.
