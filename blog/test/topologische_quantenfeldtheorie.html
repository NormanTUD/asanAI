🌀 The Big Idea

A TQFT is a bridge between shapes (topology) and numbers (quantum physics). It assigns numbers to spaces in a way that only cares about the overall "shape" — not sizes, angles, or distances.

🎮 Interactive Demo: Watch how different shapes get assigned quantum values. Hover over shapes to see their invariants. Click to morph them!

Key Insight

A coffee mug and a donut are "the same" in topology (both have one hole). TQFT assigns them the same quantum number. This is what makes it topological — it ignores geometry and only sees connectivity.

🔗 Cobordisms — The Morphisms of TQFT

A cobordism is a surface that connects two boundaries. Think of it as a "process" that transforms one set of circles into another. In TQFT, cobordisms become linear maps between vector spaces.

🎮 Click to place input circles (left) and output circles (right). Then click "Connect" to generate a cobordism between them. Try different configurations!

🧪 Pair of Pants

The most famous cobordism: 1 circle → 2 circles. This represents multiplication in the quantum algebra!

🧢 Cap & Cup

A cap (0→1) creates a state. A cup (1→0) evaluates it. Together they form the trace!

⚡ The TQFT Functor — Putting It All Together

A TQFT is formally a functor Z: Cob → Vect. It maps:

  • Closed (n-1)-manifolds → Vector spaces
  • Cobordisms (n-manifolds with boundary) → Linear maps
  • Gluing of cobordisms → Composition of maps

🏆 The Axioms (Visually!)

Functoriality: Gluing cobordisms = composing maps

Z(M₁ ∘ M₂) = Z(M₁) ∘ Z(M₂)

Monoidality: Disjoint union = tensor product

Z(Σ₁ ⊔ Σ₂) = Z(Σ₁) ⊗ Z(Σ₂)
