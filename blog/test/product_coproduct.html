A product of two objects A and B is an object A × B equipped with two projections — arrows that "extract" each component. Its universal property: for any object X with arrows to A and B, there exists a unique arrow from X to A × B making everything commute.

Key insight: Drag the X slider — no matter where X is, the unique dashed arrow always exists. The product is the "best" object that maps to both A and B. Add extra objects to see they ALL factor through A × B.

A coproduct of two objects A and B is an object A + B equipped with two injections — arrows that "include" each component. Its universal property: for any object X with arrows from A and B, there exists a unique arrow from A + B to X making everything commute.

Key insight: Notice the arrows are reversed compared to the product! Injections go INTO A+B, and the universal arrow goes OUT of A+B. This is the dual construction.

Products and coproducts are dual — one is obtained from the other by reversing all arrows. Watch both diagrams side by side and drag the slider to see the perfect mirror symmetry.

Duality principle: Every theorem about products automatically gives a theorem about coproducts (and vice versa) by reversing arrows. In Set: product = cartesian product, coproduct = disjoint union.

In the category Set, the product is the Cartesian product and the coproduct is the disjoint union. Click elements below to build a morphism and see how the universal property works concretely.
