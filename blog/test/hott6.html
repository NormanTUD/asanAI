Types vs Sets: A New Foundation

In set theory, we have sets and propositions as two separate layers. In type theory, there is only one notion: types. Click to explore the differences.

Type Theory

One basic notion: types. The judgment a : A means "a is a term of type A." Every term inherently belongs to a type — you cannot have a term without specifying its type.

Set Theory

Two layers: first-order logic + axioms about sets. Membership a ∈ A is a proposition that may or may not hold between pre-existing objects.

Judgmental Equality

a ≡ b : A — "equal by definition." This is a meta-theoretic judgment, not something you can negate or assume. It's algorithmically decidable.

Propositional Equality

a =_A b — a type! An element p : a =_A b is evidence (a witness, a path) that a and b are equal. This can be non-trivial.

a : A "a is a term of type A"

Propositions as Types

The Curry-Howard correspondence: logical propositions correspond to types, and proofs correspond to elements. To prove "A and B" is to construct a pair (a, b).

Natural Numbers and Induction

N is generated by 0 and succ. The induction principle: to prove P(n) for all n, prove P(0) and P(n) implies P(succ(n)). Watch recursion in action!

double(0) := 0
double(succ(n)) := succ(succ(double(n)))
-- double(2) equiv succ(succ(succ(succ(0)))) equiv 4

Identity Types: Paths Between Points

The identity type a =_A b is the type of paths from a to b. Paths can be composed, inverted, and form a groupoid structure.

refl_a : a = a — every point has a constant path to itself

The Big Picture

Chapter 1 builds a language where types are spaces, terms are points, and equality is paths.

Types are spaces
Terms a : A are points of A
Functions f : A -> B are continuous maps
Type families B : A -> U are fibrations
Dependent functions are sections
Identity type a =_A b is the path space
refl_a is the constant path at a
Sigma-types are total spaces
Pi-types are spaces of sections
