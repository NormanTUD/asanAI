Dynamics of the Transformer Residual Stream

How training sculpts spectral geometry and couples it to network topology in production-scale LLMs

↓

🔬 What This Paper Does

This paper treats each transformer layer as one time-step of a dynamical system. The residual stream is the state, and the per-layer Jacobian is the local linear map describing how perturbations propagate.

They compute exact Jacobians (not approximations!) for three production-scale LLMs and perform full eigendecomposition at every layer.

📐 The Non-Normality Gradient

A matrix is normal if its eigenvectors are orthogonal (like symmetric or unitary matrices). Non-normal matrices can do something surprising: transiently amplify inputs even when all eigenvalues are inside the unit circle.

The Sign Rule: Operator Type Determines Amplification Direction

Key Discovery: Boundary nodes are amplified at near-symmetric layers (high self-alignment) and de-amplified at non-normal layers (low self-alignment). This coupling is absent at initialization (0/32 FDR-significant layers) and emerges monotonically over training (→14/32 → 24/32 layers).

🎯 Summary: The Big Picture

Training sculpts the transformer into a precision instrument: early layers rotate and mix, mid layers compress, late layers read out — all through a learned non-normal geometry that funnels information into a tiny subspace.
