1. Lipschitz Lenses — The Maps ARE the Information

A Lipschitz map is a function that distorts space by at most a bounded factor. In our framework, each transformer layer applies such a map — and the distortion pattern itself encodes the model's knowledge. The lens metaphor is literal: the Lipschitz map is the lens through which scrambled topological information becomes visible. Move your mouse to position the lens and watch the scrambled topology resolve.

"The computational content of a transformer layer is more faithfully described as a deformation of the embedding space (a Lipschitz map Φ: ℝᵈ→ℝᵈ) than as a displacement of individual token vectors. The geometry of the map — its Jacobian field, eigenvalue spectrum, divergence, curl, and shear — provides indirect access to the model's reasoning." — Conjecture 1

3. Focus-Dependent Resolution — The Tradeoff in Action

The fixed dimension d limits how many islands can be sharp at once. This is the resolution–breadth tradeoff: focusing the Lipschitz lens on one region forces others to blur. Drag the lenses below and watch: sharpening Math blurs Code; sharpening Code blurs Language. The total "tile budget" is conserved — this IS the finite-dimension constraint made visible.

"Narrow attention (low entropy) yields a sharp view of few stalks; broad attention yields a blurred view of many — a resolution–breadth tradeoff enforced by the convex combination in fixed dimensionality d." — Conjecture 6

4. Layer Depth — Progressive Deholographisation

As we go deeper through layers, the Lipschitz maps progressively deholographise the scrambled topology. Early layers route (deciding WHERE to look), middle layers resolve (sharpening the topology), late layers glue (reassembling for output). The scrambled torii and loop spaces become clearer with each layer — but only in the focused region.

"Layer ℓ does not merely deform the space — it generates the instructions for layer ℓ+1. This may enable per-layer deholographisation: instead of decoding the holographic signal globally, one computes the full local chart atlas at each layer and unwinds the scrambling one layer at a time." — Conjecture 6
