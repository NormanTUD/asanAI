3. Focus-Dependent Resolution — The Tradeoff in Action

The fixed dimension d limits how many islands can be sharp at once. Drag the lenses to reposition them. When you focus more lenses on one island, it gets dramatically more detailed (more DCT tiles allocated). Others lose resolution and become pixelated/jaggy. The total tile budget is conserved.

"Narrow attention (low entropy) yields a sharp view of few stalks; broad attention yields a blurred view of many — a resolution–breadth tradeoff enforced by the convex combination in fixed dimensionality d." — Conjecture 6

Drag colored lens circles to reposition. Watch the bar chart — conservation law in action!
