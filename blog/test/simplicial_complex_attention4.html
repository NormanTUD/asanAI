The Two Operations of Attention

① Boundary Operator → Simplicial Skeleton

The inner product Q·Kᵀ defines a weighted nerve: it decides which tokens form simplices (vertices, edges, triangles). This is purely combinatorial/topological.
\[\alpha_{ij} = \text{softmax}_j\!\left(\frac{\langle R_m q_i,\; R_n k_j \rangle}{\sqrt{d}}\right)\] \[\sigma^1 = \{i,j\} \in \mathcal{K} \iff \min(\alpha_{ij}, \alpha_{ji}) > \varepsilon\] \[\sigma^2 = \{i,j,k\} \in \mathcal{K} \iff \text{all faces} \in \mathcal{K}\]

The nerve condition: a k-simplex exists iff all its (k−1)-faces exist. This is the "downward closure" axiom of simplicial complexes.

② Sheaf → Value Transport

Once the skeleton exists, V assigns a section (vector) to each vertex, and attention weights define the restriction maps that transport data along edges. The output is a global section of this sheaf.
\[\mathcal{F}(\sigma^0_i) = v_i = W^V x_i \quad \text{(stalk at vertex } i\text{)}\] \[\text{res}_{i \leftarrow j} = \alpha_{ij} \cdot v_j \quad \text{(restriction map)}\] \[s_i = \sum_{j:\{i,j\}\in\mathcal{K}} \alpha_{ij}\, v_j \quad \text{(local section / output)}\]

The attention output is literally a weighted average of sheaf stalks — a "smoothed global section" over the simplicial complex.

③ RoPE as Parallel Transport

\[R_m = \begin{pmatrix}\cos m\theta & -\sin m\theta & 0\\ \sin m\theta & \cos m\theta & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}, \quad \theta = b^{-2/d}\] \[\langle R_m q, R_n k\rangle = q^\top R_{m-n}\, k\]

RoPE is a connection on the vector bundle: it defines how to parallel-transport vectors between positions. Only relative position (m−n) matters — this is gauge invariance.

④ Why Two Things, Not Three?

Q and K are not separate objects — they are two halves of a single bilinear form:

\[B(x_i, x_j) = (W^Q x_i)^\top (W^K x_j) = x_i^\top \underbrace{(W^Q)^\top W^K}_{=:\,G} x_j\]

G is a learned metric tensor on the embedding space. Q·K is just one thing: a (possibly asymmetric) inner product that defines the topology. V is the second thing: the content that lives on that topology.

Reading Guide

Points = Stalks

Each token is a point in 3D semantic space. Its position encodes meaning along three interpretable axes. The V-projection assigns a "stalk" (local data) to each point.

Edges = Restriction Maps

Green lines connect tokens with mutual attention > ε. These are the 1-simplices. Meaning flows along them via the sheaf's restriction maps (attention weights).

Triangles = Composed Meaning

Gold triangles are 2-simplices: three tokens jointly composing meaning. This is where attention "focuses locally" — creating a coherent semantic region from multiple tokens.

The key insight: Attention is not just "soft lookup." It constructs a simplicial complex (the nerve of semantic overlap) and then solves a sheaf-consistency problem over it. The output at each token is the best "global section" — a coherent assignment of meaning that respects all the local constraints imposed by the attention edges. Triangles represent places where three meanings are being jointly reconciled, not just pairwise.
