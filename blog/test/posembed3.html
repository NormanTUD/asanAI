What you're seeing: There are 5 circles, each representing one frequency dimension of the positional encoding. The leftmost circle has the highest frequency (spins fast → encodes fine position differences). The rightmost has the lowest frequency (spins slow → encodes coarse structure) [[4]] [[10]].

Why it works: At each position, the combination of angles across all circles is unique — like clock hands. The second hand (high freq) distinguishes nearby positions. The hour hand (low freq) distinguishes distant ones. Together they give every position a distinct identity [[4]] [[9]].

The interference pattern: Each source point emits cosine ripples that decay with distance. The field you see is the sum of all ripples. Bright zones = all waves in phase (constructive). Dark zones = waves cancel (destructive). This is analogous to how the dot product between two positional encodings captures their relative distance — nearby positions share similar bright zones [[6]] [[10]].
