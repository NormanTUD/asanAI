Q, K, V — What They Actually Do

Interactive geometric intuition for transformer attention

1. Tokens live as vectors

Each word becomes a point (vector) in space. Similar words → nearby vectors.

"cat" "dog" "car"

Drag the tokens around to move them.

2. Q and K: "What am I looking for?" vs "What do I offer?"

Each token is linearly projected into a Query and a Key. These are just matrix multiplications — rotations and stretches of the space.

20° 340°
Q vectors (solid) K vectors (dashed)

The rotation controls simulate what the learned W_Q and W_K matrices do — they re-orient the space so that "relevant" tokens align.

3. Attention score = how aligned is Q with K?

The dot product q·k measures how much two vectors point the same way. High alignment → high attention.

1.4

The dashed lines show the projection of Q onto each K direction. Longer projection = higher score.

4. Softmax → attention weights (sum to 1)

1.0

Low sharpness → uniform attention. High sharpness → focuses on one token.

5. Output = weighted sum of Values

V vectors carry the "content." The output is pulled toward highly-attended V's.

60°
V vectors Output (weighted sum)

The purple star is always inside the "triangle" of V vectors — it's a convex combination.

6. The full picture

Q and K decide where to look. V decides what to read. The output blends information from all tokens, weighted by relevance.
